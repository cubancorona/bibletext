BibleText — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 26 June 2026

BibleText is a Bible reading and study app, built to respect your privacy. This policy explains what information the app does and does not handle.

The short version

BibleText has no user accounts, shows no ads, and does not collect, track, or sell your personal information. The developer runs no server that receives your data.

Information stored on your device

Your reading position, history, display settings, and any AI provider key you choose to add are stored only on your device. They are not transmitted to the developer.

Bible text

Bible translations are downloaded from public, free Bible-text services (for example bible.helloao.org and bible-api.com) so the app can display scripture. These are ordinary network requests for the text itself; no personal information about you is attached to them.

Optional AI study features (bring your own key)

BibleText's AI features — such as Find, Ask, Explain, and context or translation analysis — are optional and active only if you add your own API key for a third-party AI provider (for example Google Gemini, OpenAI, Anthropic, or xAI/Grok). When you use one of these features, the passage you selected and your question are sent directly from your device to the provider you chose, using your own key, so it can generate a response. That request is governed by that provider's own privacy policy and terms. BibleText does not route it through any server of ours and does not retain your prompts.

Children's privacy

BibleText does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy
